Data and story:

With the recent terrorist attacks by muslim extremists in Europe and the arrival of thousands of muslim refugees in Europe, the spread of islamisation has become a major concern of the Western world and many countries are calling for restrictions on muslim immigration. But how large is the muslim population really, and how much will it grow in the future?

Based on muslim population data collected by Pew research I try to answer that question by visualising the muslim population percentage of European countries. I do this by showing the muslim population in Europe in 1990, and allow the reader to jump in time to the recent past (2010), and project into the future (2030) - to see how to the muslim population in each country has changed and will likely change in the future. I allow the reader to both select specific countries and look in closer detail at the absolute population and its percentage share, allowing them to use an overview of all countries and their change in time per country.

I also interpolate the country-specific data in small multiple view to see how the top 25 muslim populations europe are (mostly) growing each year. Together, these linked views in my annoyed charts provide both a birds-eye view of the total European muslim population and a fine-grained country-per-country annual account of muslim population dynamics.
